Cookie Policy

Last Updated: March 31, 2024

This Cookie Policy explains how PersistLogic ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

This policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies serve various purposes:

  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Understanding how you use our website
  • Improving your user experience
  • Enabling certain website features and functionality
  • Analyzing website traffic and performance

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of our website. They enable basic functions like page navigation, access to secure areas, and form submissions.

Examples:

  • Session management cookies
  • Security and authentication cookies
  • Load balancing cookies

These cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the website to function properly.

2.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, including which pages are visited most often and if error messages are received.

Purpose:

  • Understand website traffic patterns
  • Identify popular content and areas for improvement
  • Measure website performance and loading times
  • Optimize user experience

Data collected is aggregated and anonymous.

2.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as language preferences or region) and provide enhanced, personalized features.

Examples:

  • Language preference cookies
  • Form data retention
  • User interface customization
  • Remembered login information (if applicable)

2.4 Targeting and Advertising Cookies

We may use these cookies to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

Purpose:

  • Deliver personalized content and ads
  • Measure ad campaign performance
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Track conversions and user behavior across different websites

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may allow trusted third-party services to set cookies on our website for the following purposes:

4.1 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our website. Google Analytics sets cookies to collect information such as:

  • Number of visitors and page views
  • Visitor behavior and navigation patterns
  • Geographic location (country/city level)
  • Device and browser information

Learn more: Google Privacy Policy

4.2 Social Media Plugins

We may use social media buttons and plugins (LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.) that may set cookies to track social sharing and interactions.

4.3 Embedded Content

Content embedded from third parties (videos, maps, etc.) may set their own cookies. We have no control over these cookies.

5. How to Control and Delete Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can control cookies through:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Clear cookies when you close your browser

How to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of certain tracking cookies using these tools:

  • Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Digital Advertising Alliance Opt-Out
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance

5.3 Mobile Devices

For mobile devices, you can control tracking through:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking → Allow Apps to Request to Track (disable)
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Please note: If you disable or delete cookies, some features of our website may not function properly. Specifically:

  • You may need to re-enter information multiple times
  • Personalized features and preferences will not be available
  • Some pages may not display correctly
  • Form submissions may not work as expected

Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled, as they are essential for the website to function.

7. Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers transmit "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals to websites. Currently, there is no industry-wide standard for responding to DNT signals. Our website does not currently respond to DNT signals, but we respect your privacy choices and provide cookie control options as described above.
